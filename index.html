His Life and Legacy

November 9th, 1934: Carl is born in Brooklyn, New York.


1951: Graduates from Rahway High School as Valedictorian.


1955: Earns Bachelor Degree in Physics from Univeristy of Chicago.


1956: Earns M.S. in Physics.


1957: Marries Biologist Lynn Margulis. The couple had two children, Jeremy and Dorion Sagan.


1958: Attains Government Security Clearance and begins work on Air Force Project A119 - to Detonate a Nuclear Device on the Moon.


1960: Earns Ph.D. degree with the dissertation Physical Studies of Planets.


1961: Begins working with NASA's Mariner 2 team, and served as a "Planetary Sciences Consultant" to the RAND Corporation. He's offered an Assistant Professorship at Harvard University.


1968: After being denied Harvard Tenure, Sagan joins Cornell Univeristy.


1970: Offered Tenured Professorship at Cornell University.


1972: Assembles 1st Physical Message sent into Space: The Golden Record.


1977: His Book, The Dragons of Eden: Speculations on the Evolution of Human Intelligence wins the Pulitzer Prize


1980: Sagan co-wrote and narrated the award-winning 13-part PBS television series, Cosmos: A Personal Voyage. It became the most widely watched series in the history of American public television. The show has been seen by over 500 million people across 60 different countries.


1981: Sagan wins Humanist of the Year award, and marries author Ann Druyan. They later had two children, Alexandra and Samuel Sagan.


1982: Publishes a petition advocating SETI in the acclaimed journal Science, signed by 70 scientists, including seven Nobel Prize winners. This signaled a tremendous increase in the respectability of a then-controversial field.


1985: His best-selling novel Contact is published.


1994: He receives the Public Welfare Medal.


1995: His book Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space is published.


December 20th, 1996:

After a two year battle with cancer,

Dr. Sagan is laid to rest at Lake View Cemetary in Ithaca, New York.
